Why are customers canceling their memberships?

Whish I knew :). But we can analyze engagement & retention metrics, feedback from users and base product decisions on them. For example, performing cohort analysis, divide customers in segments based on their “time to cancel” (i.e. cancelled after 30 days vs. canceled after more than 30 days) and focus on long-term users who have greater CLV and cancellation reasons we can address. Or we can go with churn models, calculating each customer 'Probability alive'.

What is the maximum I should pay to acquire customers?

Most shops have a 'gut feeling' of what their customer lifetime value (CLV) is, but no way of knowing for sure. Yet this is the number that would make the largest impact to their bottom line, if only it was possible to have it. Turns out CLV can be computed with surprising accuracy (around 10% error) at the individual level. We'll use sophisticated Bayesian models to estimate CLV. We'll look beyond what has happened in the past (which you get with say Google analytics).

Jose and I collaborated in the EU funded research project LarKC, building the Large Knowledge Collider, an infrastructure for very large scale reasoning with data on the Web. One of the exciting aspects of that project was the collaboration between computer scientists and cognitive scientists. Jose was absolutely crucial in bridging the gap between those two communities. I remember very well the first meeting where he attended. I assumed he was a psychologist (since he came from Max Planck Institute), but he kept asking all these hard-core techie questions! Since that first meeting, Jose has continued to impress me with his breadth of knowledge and skills that span different disciplines.
